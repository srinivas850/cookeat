Ingredients

  1. Chickpeas (chole)
  2. Onions
  3. Tomatoes
  4. Ginger
  5. Garlic
  6. Green chilies
  7. Garam masala
  8. Turmeric
  9. Cumin
  10. Coriander
  11. Chili powder
  12. Amchur (dried mango powder)
  13. Flour
  14. Yogurt
  15. Baking powder
  16. Oil
  17. Salt

Preparation

  1. Cook Chickpeas: Soak chickpeas overnight, then pressure cook with salt until tender. Drain and set aside.
  2. Prepare Chole: In a pan, heat oil and add cumin seeds. Once they splutter, add chopped onions, ginger, and garlic. Saut√© until onions are golden brown. Add chopped tomatoes, turmeric, garam masala, coriander powder, chili powder, and salt. Cook until tomatoes are soft. Add cooked chickpeas and simmer until the flavors meld together.
  3. Make Bhature Dough: In a bowl, mix flour, yogurt, baking powder, and salt. Knead into a soft dough. Cover and let it rest for 2 hours.
  4. Fry Bhature: Divide the dough into small balls and roll them out into discs. Deep-fry the discs in hot oil until they puff up and turn golden brown.
  5. Serve: Serve hot chole with freshly fried bhature, sliced onions, and lemon wedges.
